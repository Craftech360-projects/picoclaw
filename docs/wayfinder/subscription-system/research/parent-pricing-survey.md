# Parent Pricing & Packaging Survey — Instrument

> Prepared 2026-07-14 for wayfinder ticket [Parent pricing & packaging research](../tickets/008-parent-packaging-research.md).
> Run by: founder, ~15–20 Indian parents of kids aged 3–8 (mix of current Cheeko owners + prospects).
> Format: 10-minute WhatsApp voice call or in-person chat. NOT a form-blast — the "why" behind answers is half the value.
> ⚠️ Honesty note: Van Westendorp is statistically reliable at 100–300 respondents. At n≈20 this is **directional** —
> treat intersections as signal, decide with judgment. That's fine: the decision it feeds (₹499 hero + framing) needs
> confidence, not p-values.

## Recruiting

- 15–20 parents, kids aged 3–8. Aim for ≥6 current Cheeko owners (they know the value) and ≥6 prospects (they anchor like the market will).
- Mix of metro/non-metro if possible; note household language.
- Do NOT lead with "we're deciding prices" — say "we're designing plans and want your reaction".

## Script

### Intro (read aloud, neutral)

> "Cheeko is the AI toy your child talks to — it answers questions, tells stories, plays in 11 Indian languages, and you see everything in the parent app. Running the AI costs us money per conversation, so we're introducing monthly plans. Every new toy comes with the first month free. I'll show you two ways the plans could look and ask a few price questions. There are no right answers — first reactions are the most useful."

### Section A — Context (2 min)

1. Child's age? Who bought/would buy the toy — you, gifted, grandparents?
2. What do you currently pay monthly for kids' subscriptions (apps, classes, OTT kids' content)? *(anchors their reference frame — note the number)*
3. Roughly how much does your child use Cheeko (owners) / how much would they, honestly (prospects)?

### Section B — Framing A/B (3 min) ⭐ the packaging decision hangs on this

Randomize: odd-numbered respondents see **Card Q first**, even-numbered see **Card M first**.

**Card Q (questions):**
> ₹199 — 100 questions/month · ₹499 — 300 questions/month (~10 a day) · ₹999 — 800 questions/month. AI drawing included in all.

**Card M (minutes):**
> ₹199 — 60 min talk time/month (~8 min/day with caps) · ₹499 — 200 min (~15 min/day) · ₹999 — 500 min (~30 min/day). AI drawing included in all.

Ask after showing the FIRST card only:
1. "In your own words — what does the ₹499 plan get you?" *(comprehension check — note hesitation, wrong restatements)*
2. "Does that feel like enough for your child? Too little? Too much?"

Then show the second card:
3. "Same plans, described differently. Which way is clearer to you?"
4. "Which feels more generous, even though they're the same?"
5. "With questions — would you worry about your child 'using them up'? With minutes — does it feel like a phone bill?" *(probe anxiety, both directions)*

**Record: comprehension (clean/confused) per framing, stated preference, generosity perception, anxiety notes.**

### Section C — Van Westendorp on the hero plan (2 min)

Frame: "Think about the middle plan — daily use for your child, whichever description you preferred."

1. At what monthly price would it be **so expensive** you wouldn't consider it?
2. At what price would it be **so cheap** you'd doubt the quality?
3. At what price does it start feeling **expensive, but you might still consider it**?
4. At what price is it a **bargain**?

*(Open numbers, don't suggest. Record all four.)*

### Section D — Purchase intent (Gabor-Granger, 2 min)

1. "Would you actually subscribe at **₹499/month**?" (yes / probably / no)
   - If **no/probably** → "At **₹299**?" → if no → "What number works?"
   - If **yes** → "At **₹699**?" *(tests headroom)*
2. "Annual option: pay for 10 months, get 12 — **₹4,990/year**. Would you take annual or stay monthly?" *(UPI Autopay comfort matters here — note any autopay hesitation)*

### Section E — The conversion moment (1 min)

> "Imagine the free first month just ended. Your child asks for Cheeko in the morning and it says 'ask Mumma to check the app'. You open the app and see the three plans. What do you honestly do?"

- Pick one: Starter ₹199 / Family ₹499 / Premium ₹999 / "let the toy stay quiet for now"
- "Why that one?"
- (Owners only) "What would make it a no-brainer?"

## Analysis plan → feeds [Packaging decision](../tickets/010-packaging-decision.md)

| Question | Decision rule (directional) |
|---|---|
| Framing winner | Framing with cleaner comprehension (Q B1) AND majority preference (B3). If comprehension and preference split, comprehension wins — a plan parents misunderstand generates support tickets. |
| ₹499 hero validation | VW: does ₹499 sit inside the OPP–IDP zone (plot the 4 cumulative curves, even hand-drawn)? Gabor-Granger: ≥50% yes/probably at ₹499. If both fail toward ₹299, revisit tier prices before schema work. |
| Annual appetite | ≥30% picking annual justifies building annual at launch; below that, annual can ship later (one less Razorpay plan object per tier). |
| Conversion moment | Modal pick at Section E is the de-facto hero plan — if it's Starter, the ₹499 anchor needs rework (tier gap too big or value story weak). |
